During the analysis of publicly available job postings, several important workforce and training-related data gaps were identified:

1. Employee Training Programs – Job postings mention required skills but rarely describe the company's internal training or onboarding programs.

2. Training Budgets – Information on how much organizations invest in employee learning and development is generally not publicly available.

3. Training Effectiveness – Public job listings do not provide data on training completion rates, employee performance improvements, or return on investment (ROI) from training initiatives.

4. Skill Gap Assessments – Companies specify desired skills but do not indicate the current skill gaps among their workforce or the areas requiring additional development.

5. Career Progression After Training – There is limited public information on how training impacts employee promotions, retention, or long-term career growth.

6. Technology Adoption Levels – While job descriptions mention tools such as ERP systems, warehouse operations, and automation, they do not reveal the actual level of technology adoption within the organization.

7. Workforce Performance Metrics – Operational indicators such as productivity, efficiency improvements, employee satisfaction, and training outcomes are not available through public job postings.
